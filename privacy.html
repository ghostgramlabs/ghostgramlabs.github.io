Website Privacy & Cookies

The short version: this website counts visits with Google Analytics if you say yes, uses no ads, and never sells anything about you. Here are the details, in plain language.

Last updated: July 10, 2026

Who runs this site

GhostGram Labs is not a company — it's the name an independent developer publishes free Android side projects under. You can reach me anytime at ghostgramlabs@gmail.com.

What this website collects

If you press "Sure, count me" on the cookie banner, this site uses Google Analytics 4 to understand how it's being used. That means Google collects, on my behalf:

  • Which pages you visit and how you arrived (e.g., from a Google search)
  • Your approximate location (country and city, estimated from your IP address — never your exact position)
  • Your device and browser type

I see this data only in aggregate ("120 visitors this week, mostly from India and the US") — I cannot identify you personally, and I don't try to.

If you press "No thanks", Google Analytics does not set cookies and your visit is not tracked. The site works exactly the same either way.

Cookies

With your consent, Google Analytics sets cookies (such as _ga) to tell returning visitors apart from new ones. Your consent choice itself is remembered in your browser's local storage — that's a setting on your device, not a tracking cookie. No advertising cookies are used, ever.

What this website does NOT do

  • No ads, no ad networks, no remarketing
  • No selling or sharing of data with anyone besides Google Analytics processing described above
  • No accounts, no forms, no newsletter — this site doesn't even have a way to ask for your name

Third-party services

Google Analytics processes visit data as described above (see Google's Privacy Policy). Analytics data is retained for up to 14 months, then deleted. Google Fonts serves this site's typefaces, which means your browser requests font files from Google's servers (your IP address is visible to Google in that request, as with any web resource). The site is hosted on GitHub Pages, which may log requests for security and operations (see GitHub's Privacy Statement).

Weather effect: to show rain or snow on the page matching your local weather, your browser asks ipwho.is for your approximate location (estimated from your IP address) and Open-Meteo for the current weather there. This happens in your browser, is used only to pick the animation, and nothing about it is stored by this site. If either request fails or is blocked, the page simply shows its default animation.

Your choices and rights

  • Change your cookie choice — this reopens the consent banner
  • Block analytics entirely with the Google Analytics opt-out add-on or your browser's tracking protection
  • If you're in the EU/EEA, UK, or a region with similar laws: you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of personal data, and to withdraw consent at any time. Since I can't identify individual visitors in the aggregate data, the practical route is to email me at ghostgramlabs@gmail.com and I'll do everything I can to help.

Changes

If how this website handles data ever changes, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date revised. There are no plans to add anything beyond what's described here.
