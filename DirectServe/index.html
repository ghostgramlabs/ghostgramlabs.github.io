Why DirectServe?

Moving files between devices is still weirdly hard in 2026. AirDrop doesn't talk to Android. USB cables need drivers. Cloud drives need uploads, accounts and good internet. Most "sharing" apps demand that both devices install them.

DirectServe takes a simpler approach: your Android phone becomes a local file server. Any device on the same WiFi network or connected to your hotspot can open a link in its browser and instantly browse, preview and download whatever you chose to share — videos, photos, music, PDFs, documents, archives, anything.

Because everything happens locally, transfers are fast, work without internet, and your files never touch a cloud server. Share with an iPhone at a family gathering, pull videos to your PC without a cable, or stream media to a smart TV via DLNA — all from one app that only needs to be installed on your phone.

DirectServe is a non-profit side project — completely free with no ads, built so people have a simple file-transfer tool that just works.

FAQ

How do I send files from Android to iPhone without AirDrop?
Start sharing in DirectServe, then open the link or scan the QR code in Safari on the iPhone. Files download directly over WiFi or hotspot — no iOS app, no cloud, no cables.
Does the other device need to install anything?
No. Any device with a web browser — iPhone, iPad, Windows PC, Mac, Linux, Chromebook, tablet or smart TV — can receive files. Nothing to install on their side.
Are my files uploaded to the cloud?
Never. DirectServe works entirely on your local network. Files travel device-to-device and no internet connection or account is needed.
Can other devices send files to my phone?
Yes — two-way transfer lets others upload files to your phone from their browser, with optional PIN protection and upload approval so you stay in control.

Stop emailing files to yourself

DirectServe is free on Google Play. One install on your phone covers every device you own.
